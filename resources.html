Living Together

Multi-generational households and the how tos:

Photo by August de Richelieu from Pexels

Living together is an intimidating concept especially for families rooted in traditionally nuclear families (both parents, kids and pets). This is why we created pedigree. We want the public understanding and transition into multigenerational living to be as informative, comfortable and seamless as can be. We have considered the many benefits and downsides of living together so you didn’t have to.

Space. Studies of anthropology in ethnic civillations revealed that the success of living together is a result of members having a sense of physical space and sometimes emotional space.

time investments. to attain the gains/benefits of living together under one roof requires certain levels of time investments of and to each member around.

Order: Roles and Responsibilities. By setting clear boundaries in understanding the roles of each understanding member within units. It helps maintain organization and less chaos within households.

the pro
  • increased bonding
  • financial advancements / more money available to be allocated
  • emotional safety-net
  • general better nutritional standing of children
  • less risk of infant mortality
  • increased child development
  • caregiving for senior adults and kids
  • overall convenience
  • likelihood of increased matrilineages and matriarchs
the con
  • disagreements
  • lack of autonomy / boundaries
  • sense of restricted freedom
  • conflict over gendering, sexuality & relationships
  • varying parenting styles
(Additonal Source : Cohn, D’Vera. Horowitz, Juliana, et al. “Financial Issues Top the List of Reasons U.S. Adults Live in Multigenerational Homes.” Retrieved Oct. 9th, 2022.)
